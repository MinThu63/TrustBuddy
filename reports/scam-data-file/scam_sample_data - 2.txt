SCAM ADVISORY DATABASE - BATCH 002
Scam Category: Government Officials Impersonation Scams Risk Level: CRITICAL Target Demographic: Elderly and General Public Common Phrases Used: "This is Inspector Lim. Transfer your money to our safety account to assist in our investigation now!" Modus Operandi: Scammers call you pretending to be police officers or government officials. They will make fake accusations about crimes and tell you to transfer your savings to a "safety account" to clear your name. Safety Advice: Government officers and police will never ask you to transfer your money to any account or ask for your bank password over the phone. If someone calls you claiming to be a police officer and asks for money, hang up immediately and tell your children or call the police yourself.
Scam Category: Investment Scams Risk Level: CRITICAL Target Demographic: General Public Common Phrases Used: "Guaranteed high return investment!", "No risk, fast cash" Modus Operandi: Scammers lure victims on social media with fake promises of massive, guaranteed returns. They direct you to transfer your funds to unverified sources or bank accounts. Safety Advice: If someone online offers you a way to make quick, guaranteed money with zero risk, it is always a trick. Never transfer your hard-earned money to anyone you have never met for "investments." Always check directly with your official bank or a licensed local broker.
Scam Category: Job Scams Risk Level: CRITICAL Target Demographic: Job Seekers and General Public Common Phrases Used: "EASY JOB! Earn S$50 by completing 2 simple market surveys!" Modus Operandi: Scammers post too-good-to-be-true job offers online. They pay you a tiny bit of money at first to trust them, then ask you to pay your own money first as a "deposit" to get more jobs. Safety Advice: Real companies will never ask you to pay money first to get a job or to get your salary. If an online job offers easy money for simple tasks but asks you to pay first, block the contact immediately.
Scam Category: E-Commerce Scams Risk Level: CRITICAL Target Demographic: Online Shoppers Common Phrases Used: "Cheap deal, buy now", "Pay deposit to reserve the item" Modus Operandi: Scammers advertise products at extremely cheap prices on online platforms. They tell you to pay immediately via direct bank transfer or PayLah before they send the item, then disappear with your money. Safety Advice: Do not buy items from strange online sellers who refuse face-to-face meetups or cash-on-delivery. Only shop on trusted platforms, and never transfer money directly to a seller's personal bank account before receiving your item.
Scam Category: Phishing Scams (General) Risk Level: HIGH Target Demographic: General Public Common Phrases Used: "Your account is locked", "Verify your identity immediately", "Click the link to update your details" Modus Operandi: Scammers send fake SMSes, emails, or call you pretending to be from your bank or government agencies. They trick you into clicking links that lead to fake websites designed to steal your banking usernames, passwords, and security codes. Safety Advice: Banks in Singapore will never send you SMSes or emails containing clickable links. If a message has a link telling you to log in to your bank account, delete it immediately. Always use your official bank application on your phone.
Scam Category: Fake Friend Call Scams Risk Level: CRITICAL Target Demographic: Elderly Common Phrases Used: "HELLO, REMEMBER ME? I'M YOUR FRIEND!" Modus Operandi: A stranger calls you claiming to be an old friend who has changed their phone number. Once you guess a name, they pretend to be that friend and ask to borrow money urgently for a financial crisis. Safety Advice: If someone calls you from a strange number and says "remember me?", do not guess their name. Hang up the phone and call your actual friend using the old phone number you already have saved in your phonebook to verify.
Scam Category: Loan Scams Risk Level: CRITICAL Target Demographic: General Public Common Phrases Used: "FAST CASH LOANS! ZERO INTEREST!" Modus Operandi: Scammers send SMSes or WhatsApp messages offering quick, easy loans with low or zero interest. Before giving you the loan, they demand that you pay "admin fees" or "processing charges" upfront, then disappear. Safety Advice: Licensed moneylenders in Singapore are strictly not allowed to send unsolicited loan advertisements via SMS or WhatsApp. They will also never ask you to pay any money upfront before releasing a loan. If you need a loan, go directly to a real bank.
Scam Category: Tech Support Scams Risk Level: HIGH Target Demographic: Elderly and General Public Common Phrases Used: "WARNING! Your system had been compromised. Call technical support immediately! +65 6964(XXXX)" Modus Operandi: Fake security warning windows pop up on your computer screen claiming your system is hacked or infected. When you call the number, the scammer asks you to download software that lets them control your computer and drain your bank account. Safety Advice: If your computer shows a scary warning screen telling you to call a number, do not call it. Turn off your computer completely and turn it back on. Never give remote control of your device to a stranger who calls you.
Scam Category: Insurance Service Scams Risk Level: CRITICAL Target Demographic: General Public Common Phrases Used: "Your insurance policy has expired", "Pay outstanding premium immediately" Modus Operandi: Scammers contact you pretending to represent major insurance companies. They falsely claim your medical or life insurance policy has lapsed and demand immediate cash payment to keep your coverage active. Safety Advice: Never make any payments or share card details over a phone call or through links sent by SMS. Always hang up and call your insurance agent directly or use the official hotline number listed on your policy documents to check.
Scam Category: Internet Love Scams Risk Level: CRITICAL Target Demographic: General Public Common Phrases Used: "I love you", "I want to spend my life with you" Modus Operandi: Scammers create fake, attractive profiles on social media and build an online relationship with you. Once they win your heart, they invent sad stories or claim to have sent you expensive gifts held at customs, begging you to transfer money to help them. Safety Advice: Never send money or share your bank details with anyone you have only met online, no matter how much they say they love you. Real online friends or lovers will never ask you for money or bank transfers.
Scam Category: Sexual Service Scams Risk Level: CRITICAL Target Demographic: General Public Common Phrases Used: "Special massage services", "Buy gift cards for payment" Modus Operandi: Scammers advertise escort or massage services on social media. They demand upfront payment in the form of shopping gift cards or online credits, and may blackmail you for more money once they have your details. Safety Advice: Do not look for sexual or massage services on social media or messaging apps. If someone asks you to buy iTunes or Google Play gift cards to pay for a service, it is always a scam. Never pay anyone who threatens to expose you.
Scam Category: DBS Token Activation Phishing Scams (June 2026 Email) Risk Level: HIGH Target Demographic: DBS and POSB Bank Customers Common Phrases Used: "DBS Security Alert", "SECURE YOUR DIGIBANK MOBILE ACCESS", "including Digital Token access on a single registered mobile device" Modus Operandi: Scammers send phishing emails claiming to be from "Team from iToken DBS/POSB" with sender addresses like affiliates@rewardful.com. The email urges you to click a button to secure your digibank mobile access, which takes you to a fake website that steals your login details and OTPs. Safety Advice: DBS will never send you an email with clickable links or buttons asking you to log in. Do not click any links in emails. If you need to manage your digital token, always open your official DBS digibank app directly.
Scam Category: DBS Token Activation Phishing Scams (March 2026 Email) Risk Level: HIGH Target Demographic: DBS and POSB Bank Customers Common Phrases Used: "Digital Token Expired", "Update your digital token", "To ensure uninterrupted access to your DBS Digital Banking services" Modus Operandi: Scammers send emails pretending to be DBS no-reply alerts from strange domains like www.thinkific.com. They tell you your digital or physical token has expired and your transactions will be limited, directing you to a button to "Update your digital token" to steal your credentials. Safety Advice: If you get an email saying your bank token is expired and has a button to click, ignore it. Banks never send emails with buttons or links to update your tokens. Open your official DBS app on your phone to check if your token is working.
Scam Category: DBS Token Activation Phishing Scams (January 2026 Email) Risk Level: HIGH Target Demographic: DBS and POSB Bank Customers Common Phrases Used: "DBS Banking Alert: Token Activation Required", "Your POSB Digital/physical token has expired", "Activate Your DBS Digital Token" Modus Operandi: Scammers send phishing emails from fake addresses like noreply@ssssssss-e9a4b.firebaseapp.com or information@ckoneh.com. The email warns that your token has expired and you must click "Activate Your DBS Digital Token" to continue your banking services. Safety Advice: Never trust emails from strange-looking addresses that claim your bank token has expired. DBS will never send you emails with direct buttons to activate tokens. If you are worried, call the official DBS Fraud Hotline at 1800 339 6963.
Scam Category: DBS Phishing Scams (Fake Website sg.pods-kyc.com) Risk Level: HIGH Target Demographic: DBS and POSB Bank Customers Common Phrases Used: "Security Verification", "Drag the key to the lock to continue", "User ID", "PIN" Modus Operandi: Scammers direct you to a fake banking login screen at an address like "sg.pods-kyc.com". The page displays a fake security verification asking you to drag a key to a lock before prompting you to enter your login ID and PIN. Safety Advice: Before entering your bank details online, always look at the address bar at the top of your screen. The official website must end in "dbs.com.sg" or "posb.com.sg". If the web address looks strange, close the page immediately.
Scam Category: DBS Phishing Scams (Fake Website irement.tempurl.host) Risk Level: HIGH Target Demographic: DBS Bank Customers Common Phrases Used: "DO NOT log into your account for the next 12 hours.", "Your Digital Token is currently being activated and requires this waiting period" Modus Operandi: Scammers lure you to a fake website "irement.tempurl.host" where you enter your banking credentials. To prevent you from realizing your money is being stolen, the fake site shows a message instructing you not to log in for 12 hours under threat of account suspension. Safety Advice: If a banking website ever tells you to stay out of your account or not to log in for a few hours, it is a scam. This is a trick so you do not see them stealing your money. Log into your official app immediately to check your balance and freeze your cards if necessary.
Scam Category: DBS Deepfake Scams (Leadership Investment Ads) Risk Level: CRITICAL Target Demographic: Investors and General Public Common Phrases Used: "A project that guarantees an income of S$3,900 SGD per month", "approved by DBS", "become financially stronger" Modus Operandi: Scammers use deepfake technology to create highly realistic fake videos of DBS senior leadership or staff on social media. These videos falsely claim that DBS has approved a project that guarantees daily or monthly income, directing you to fake websites like codevision8.pro to transfer money. Safety Advice: Even if you see a video of a famous bank CEO or government leader on Facebook promising easy money, do not believe it. Scammers can make fake videos that look and sound real. Always check directly with the official bank before investing.
Scam Category: DBS Deepfake Scams (Fake Affiliate Course) Risk Level: HIGH Target Demographic: General Public Common Phrases Used: "Systematic Investment & Financial Analysis Course (Free)", "inspired by the professional principles of DBS Bank", "Jessica Roset Durse" Modus Operandi: Scammers create fake pages on social media using personal names and run ads for free investment courses supposedly affiliated with DBS. Clicking the link takes you to a phishing site designed to collect your personal information and banking credentials. Safety Advice: DBS does not host investment courses on personal social media pages. Never click on social media ads for free courses that ask you to register with your banking details. If you want to learn about finance, go to the official bank website.
Scam Category: DBS Deepfake Scams (Government-Supported Earning Scheme) Risk Level: CRITICAL Target Demographic: General Public Common Phrases Used: "A NEW GOVERNMENT-SUPPORTED PROJECT HAS LAUNCHED", "BE ONE OF THE FIRST TO EARN FROM 25,000 SGD PER MONTH", "Let's make your first deposit together" Modus Operandi: Scammers create a fake news website (such as kaelitron.com or modmountltd.ai) claiming a new government-supported project with DBS will make citizens rich. The page prompts you to enter your personal details and credit card information to make a "secure deposit." Safety Advice: The government and local banks will never run projects that guarantee to make you rich or ask for your credit card details to register. If a website asks for your credit card number to join a "project," leave the website immediately.
Scam Category: Cryptocurrency Related Scams (OCBC/General) Risk Level: CRITICAL Target Demographic: General Public and Crypto Investors Common Phrases Used: "Private crypto sale ending now.", "Buy low, double your money fast", "Last chance!" Modus Operandi: Scammers send unsolicited messages or social media ads offering private cryptocurrency sales or high-return crypto platforms. They pressure you to make a deposit immediately due to fake deadlines, then lock you out of your account and steal your funds. Safety Advice: Cryptocurrency is extremely risky and not protected like money in a bank. Never buy crypto from links in SMSes or messages from strangers. If someone promises you can double your money quickly with crypto, block them.
Scam Category: OCBC Phishing Scams (Account Closure SMS) Risk Level: HIGH Target Demographic: OCBC Bank Customers Common Phrases Used: "OCBC: Your account function has been closed.", "You need to confirm the device immediately.", "Follow OCBC-Person.com" Modus Operandi: Scammers send fake SMSes pretending to be from OCBC claiming your account is closed or suspended. They urge you to click a link to a fake website like "OCBC-Person.com" to "confirm your device," stealing your credentials. Safety Advice: OCBC and other Singapore banks will never send you SMSes with clickable links. If you receive an SMS with a link claiming your account is closed, do not click it. Call the official OCBC hotline at 6363 3333 to verify.
Scam Category: OCBC Phishing Scams (Fake Reward/Giveaway Call) Risk Level: HIGH Target Demographic: General Public Common Phrases Used: "You have won a reward!", "You won our monthly giveaway!", "Verify your identity to get the prize" Modus Operandi: Scammers call you claiming you have won an attractive prize or cash giveaway from OCBC that you never entered. When you question this, they claim a friend entered on your behalf and ask for your NRIC and bank credentials to "verify your identity." Safety Advice: If you did not sign up for a contest, you cannot win it. Never give your NRIC, bank login ID, or passwords over the phone to anyone claiming you won a prize. Hang up immediately.
Scam Category: OCBC Phishing Scams (Tax Rebates/Surprise Inheritance Surveys) Risk Level: HIGH Target Demographic: General Public Common Phrases Used: "You have an outstanding tax rebate", "Claim your surprise inheritance", "Fill out this survey for a prize" Modus Operandi: Scammers send messages offering unexpected money like tax rebates from IRAS or surprise inheritances. They instruct you to fill out an online survey or form which is secretly designed to steal your bank login credentials. Safety Advice: Real government agencies like IRAS and banks will never ask you for your login passwords or PINs to give you a tax rebate. Never fill out online surveys or forms that ask for your private banking details.
Scam Category: OCBC Phishing Scams (Government Impersonation) Risk Level: CRITICAL Target Demographic: General Public Common Phrases Used: "This is from the Ministry of Health", "Your Singpass is compromised", "You must verify your details to avoid arrest" Modus Operandi: Scammers call or message you pretending to be from government bodies like the Ministry of Health (MOH) or police. They use scare tactics, claiming your Singpass is compromised, and direct you to log in to a fake site to verify your identity. Safety Advice: Government agencies will never ask for your Singpass password, bank PIN, or credit card details over the phone or via links. If you get a suspicious government call, hang up and call their official helpline to check.
Scam Category: OCBC Phishing Scams (E-commerce and Delivery Impersonation) Risk Level: HIGH Target Demographic: Online Shoppers Common Phrases Used: "Your parcel delivery has failed", "Pay S$2 outstanding shipping fee", "Update your address to receive shipment" Modus Operandi: Scammers send fake messages pretending to be from delivery services like SingPost. They claim your package cannot be delivered because of an address mistake or a small unpaid fee, directing you to a website that steals your credit card details. Safety Advice: If you did not buy anything online, ignore any parcel delivery messages. If you are expecting a package, check the status directly on the official app or website where you bought it, never through links in messages.
Scam Category: OCBC Phishing Scams (Seasonal Events) Risk Level: HIGH Target Demographic: General Public Common Phrases Used: "Great Singapore Sale discounts!", "Chinese New Year red packet giveaway!", "National Day lucky draw!" Modus Operandi: Scammers exploit local holidays or shopping events to send messages offering free red packets, shopping vouchers, or lucky draws. These messages contain links that lead to fake banking portals asking you to log in to claim your gift. Safety Advice: Banks will never ask you to log in through an SMS or email link to receive a holiday gift or red packet. If you want to check if a promotion is real, log into your official bank app directly.
Scam Category: UOB Card Fraud (Unauthorized Transactions) Risk Level: CRITICAL Target Demographic: UOB Card Holders Common Phrases Used: "Unauthorised card transaction detected", "Lock your card immediately", "Call UOB Fraud Hotline" Modus Operandi: This occurs when your credit or debit card details have been compromised online and unauthorized transactions are made. You can immediately stop further transactions by locking your card in the bank's mobile app. Safety Advice: Regularly check your bank messages and transactions. If you see a charge you did not make, open your UOB TMRW app, select your card, and tap "Lock card" immediately. Then call the UOB Fraud Hotline at 6255 0160 to report it.
Scam Category: UOB Bank Account Fraud (Kill Switch Option) Risk Level: HIGH Target Demographic: UOB Online Banking Customers Common Phrases Used: "Press 4 to activate Kill Switch", "Disable your digital access and block all your cards" Modus Operandi: If you realize you have shared your bank details with scammers or clicked on a suspicious link, you can activate the bank's Kill Switch. This instantly terminates all digital logins and blocks all your credit and debit cards to protect your funds. Safety Advice: If you accidentally gave your bank password or clicked a suspicious link, call the UOB Fraud Hotline at 6255 0160 immediately and press '4' to activate the Kill Switch. This will lock your account and cards instantly so scammers cannot touch your money.
Scam Category: UOB Bank Account Fraud (Disable Digital Access) Risk Level: HIGH Target Demographic: UOB Online Banking Customers Common Phrases Used: "Press 2 to disable digital access", "Enter NRIC to confirm" Modus Operandi: When you suspect your online banking account is compromised but your physical cards are safe, you can choose to disable online access only. This option terminates any active digital banking sessions and prevents online transfers. Safety Advice: If you think someone has hacked your online banking, call UOB immediately at 6255 0160 and press '2'. Type in your NRIC number to lock your online account instantly so the hacker is kicked out.
Scam Category: UOB Bank Account Fraud (Block Cards Hotline) Risk Level: HIGH Target Demographic: UOB Debit and Credit Card Holders Common Phrases Used: "Press 3 to block all your UOB Debit/Credit cards", "Confirm card block" Modus Operandi: If you lose your physical bank cards or suspect they have been compromised, you can use the automated hotline to block them. This blocks all your physical debit and credit cards immediately to prevent offline or online transactions. Safety Advice: If you lose your UOB card or think someone has stolen your card number, call UOB's hotline at 6255 0160 right away and press '3'. Enter your NRIC number to freeze all your cards so nobody can use them to buy things.
Scam Category: Erroneous Transactions (E-Payments User Protection) Risk Level: MEDIUM Target Demographic: General Public Common Phrases Used: "Unauthorised transaction", "Erroneous transaction", "Report transaction within policy limits" Modus Operandi: This occurs when you accidentally transfer money to the wrong bank account or mobile number, or discover an unauthorized transaction on your account. The E-Payments User Protection Guidelines protect you from isolated losses if you report the error to the bank promptly. Safety Advice: If you accidentally transfer money to the wrong phone number or account, do not wait. Call your bank's fraud helpline immediately to report the error so they can assist in recovering the funds.
