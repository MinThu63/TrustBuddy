Senior Official, Royalty or Celebrity ImpersonationScam Category: Senior Official, Royalty or Celebrity Impersonation  Risk Level: HIGH  Target Demographic: Elderly users, social media users, people seeking investments or assistance  Common Phrases Used: "I am contacting you on behalf of a senior official", "You have been specially selected", "Please keep this private", "Pay an administrative fee before receiving the benefit."  Modus Operandi: Scammers impersonate a senior government official, member of royalty, or celebrity using social media, messaging apps, or calls. They build trust with you through their fake status and authority, then request money, personal details, or participation in a supposed opportunity.  Safety Advice: Do not send money or personal information. Verify the person's identity through independently located official channels, and block and report the fake account.  WhatsApp Account TakeoverScam Category: WhatsApp Account Takeover  Risk Level: CRITICAL  Target Demographic: WhatsApp users, especially elderly and less tech-confident users  Common Phrases Used: "Please send me the 6-digit code you just received", "I entered your number by mistake", "Scan this QR code to join the group", "Help me verify my account."  Modus Operandi: Scammers trick you into sharing your WhatsApp verification code, scanning a malicious linking QR code, or approving access to the account. Once they take over your account, they impersonate you and ask your contacts for money or further codes.  Safety Advice: Never share your WhatsApp verification codes or scan account-linking QR codes sent by others. Enable two-step verification. If compromised, re-register your number immediately and alert your contacts.  Telegram Social Media ImpersonationScam Category: Telegram Social Media Impersonation  Risk Level: HIGH  Target Demographic: Telegram users, friends and relatives of compromised or copied accounts  Common Phrases Used: "Can you help me buy a gift card?", "I need an urgent PayNow transfer", "Vote for me using this link", "Send me the screenshot after payment."  Modus Operandi: Scammers create or take over a Telegram account resembling someone you know. Relying on the familiar name and image, they ask you for money, gift cards, votes, login codes, or access to a link.  Safety Advice: Check the exact username and contact the person through another channel to verify. Never share login codes and do not transfer money based only on a chat message.  CDC, SG60 or Government Payout PhishingScam Category: CDC, SG60 or Government Payout Phishing  Risk Level: CRITICAL  Target Demographic: Singapore residents, elderly users and government-benefit recipients  Common Phrases Used: "Claim your CDC Vouchers now", "Your SG60 Voucher is ready", "Apply for your GST Voucher cash payout", "Redeem before the link expires."  Modus Operandi: Scammers use Telegram channels, Instagram accounts, or messages to pretend to represent government schemes. They direct you to a fake website that requests your name, NRIC number, mobile number, banking details, or Singpass credentials.  Safety Advice: Do not use links from social media or forwarded messages. Access government schemes only through official government websites, LifeSG, or verified channels, and never provide an OTP to claim a voucher.  Alipay Customer Service ImpersonationScam Category: Alipay Customer Service Impersonation  Risk Level: CRITICAL  Target Demographic: Chinese-speaking residents, Alipay users, elderly smartphone users  Common Phrases Used: "Your Alipay insurance service has been activated", "A monthly fee will be charged", "Contact customer service immediately to cancel", "Share your screen so we can process the cancellation."  Modus Operandi: Scammers call impersonating an Alipay representative, telling you a subscription or insurance service has been activated. They may transfer you to a fake bank officer, request screen sharing, or instruct you to transfer money to avoid charges.  Safety Advice: End the call and contact Alipay through its official application. Do not install remote-access software, share your screen, or transfer money based on telephone instructions.  WeChat, UnionPay or Chinese Platform ImpersonationScam Category: WeChat, UnionPay or Chinese Platform Impersonation  Risk Level: CRITICAL  Target Demographic: Chinese-speaking users, migrants, elderly users and users of Chinese payment applications  Common Phrases Used: "Your WeChat Pay account is linked to an investigation", "Your UnionPay service will be charged", "Cancel the service through our officer", "Transfer the funds for verification."  Modus Operandi: Scammers impersonate representatives from Chinese platforms like WeChat or UnionPay to create fear about unauthorized subscriptions or criminal investigations. They direct you to fake customer service agents who demand money or account access.  Safety Advice: Do not follow instructions given during the call. Verify claims through the official application or published customer service numbers, and never disclose OTPs or banking passwords.  Government Official Scam Involving Gold or ValuablesScam Category: Government Official Scam Involving Gold or Valuables  Risk Level: CRITICAL  Target Demographic: Elderly victims, retirees and people with significant savings  Common Phrases Used: "Your money is under investigation", "Withdraw cash and buy gold bars", "The valuables must be inspected", "Leave the package at the stated location", "Do not tell your family."  Modus Operandi: Scammers impersonate government officials and accuse you of criminal activity. They instruct you to withdraw money, purchase gold bars or gather valuables, and hand them over to a stranger or leave them at a hidden location for "investigation".  Safety Advice: Do not withdraw money, buy gold, or meet the collector. Government agencies do not collect valuables for investigations. Call the Police or ScamShield at 1799 using a separate phone.  Shell Investment Application ScamScam Category: Shell Investment Application Scam  Risk Level: CRITICAL  Target Demographic: Retirees, working adults and inexperienced investors  Common Phrases Used: "Download our private investment application", "Invest in a guaranteed oil project", "Transfer using this YouTrip QR code", "Hand cash or gold to our authorised representative."  Modus Operandi: Scammers promote fake investment opportunities using unverified applications or online groups. They ask you to transfer funds to random bank accounts, pay via QR codes, or hand over cash/gold, using fake dashboards as "proof" of your investment.  Safety Advice: Do not download unverified investment apps or hand money to unknown representatives. Always check the company and representative using official MAS resources before investing.  Cryptocurrency Investment Relationship ScamScam Category: Cryptocurrency Investment Relationship Scam  Risk Level: CRITICAL  Target Demographic: Dating-app users, social-media users and first-time cryptocurrency investors  Common Phrases Used: "I can teach you to invest in crypto", "My relative has insider information", "Start with a small amount", "Pay the tax before withdrawing your profit."  Modus Operandi: Scammers build a relationship with you through social media or dating apps before introducing you to a cryptocurrency investment. You are directed to a fake platform showing fabricated profits, and they demand extra deposits, taxes, or fees before you can withdraw.  Safety Advice: Do not invest based on advice from an online acquaintance. Check the platform independently and never pay an additional fee just to release supposed profits.  Malware-enabled APK ScamScam Category: Malware-enabled APK Scam  Risk Level: CRITICAL  Target Demographic: Android smartphone users, elderly users and online shoppers  Common Phrases Used: "Install our app to place the order", "Android security blocked the file – enable unknown sources", "Update the application using this APK", "Allow accessibility permission to continue."  Modus Operandi: Scammers persuade you to download an Android APK file from a website or chat instead of an official app store. The malicious app requests accessibility or screen-control permissions, letting scammers steal your credentials and make banking transactions.  Safety Advice: Install applications only from official app stores and never grant accessibility or screen-control permissions to unknown apps. If installed, disconnect your device from the internet, uninstall the app, and contact your bank immediately.
